# Rotational Transfer Sleeves

## Overview

Rotational Transfer Sleeves enable pods to move between the non-rotating spine and rotating rings.

They are mounted externally at ring connection nodes.

---

## Core Function

- isolate a section of track
- rotate it to match ring velocity
- align with rotating spoke
- release pod into ring

---

## Structure

Each sleeve consists of:

- cylindrical rotating housing
- internal detachable track segment
- alignment interface (spine side)
- alignment interface (ring side)
- high-capacity bearing system

---

## Operation Sequence

1. Pod is diverted from spine onto branch track
2. Pod enters transfer sleeve
3. Track segment is isolated from spine
4. Sleeve rotates to match ring angular velocity
5. Exit aligns with spoke track
6. Pod continues into ring

---

## Key Properties

- no doors or sealed chambers required
- alignment-based transfer
- spine remains unaffected
- multiple sleeves operate in parallel

---

## Placement

- mounted on outer surface of spine
- positioned at ring hubs
- typically 2–6 per ring depending on traffic

---

## Redundancy

- multiple sleeves per node
- independent operation
- allows continuous throughput

---

## Terminology

Standard term:

Rotational Transfer Sleeve

---

## Notes

Sleeves are treated as routine infrastructure.

Their operation is assumed continuous and stable.
```

# Transit Couplings

## Function

Transfer pods between non-rotating spine and rotating rings.

---

## Operation

1. pod diverted from spine
2. enters coupling
3. track segment isolated
4. rotates to match ring
5. aligns with spoke
6. pod exits into ring

---

## Properties

- external to spine
- no doors required
- multiple per ring
- continuous operation
```
