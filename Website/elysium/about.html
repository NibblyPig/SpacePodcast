Station Elysium — Project Information

About

What Elysium is, what kind of listening it offers, and where to begin.

The Project

Elysium is a serialised sequence of audio broadcasts from SIMON, the Station Interactive Monitor that oversees the space station Elysium. Each episode is one cycle's transmission, comprising of announcements concerning events aboard, safety notices, logistics announcements, and maintenance information.

It is unknown how long the station has been in continuous operation. Nothing is known about the whereabouts of the station. The only data we have is these transmissions. It is unknown why the transmissions begin at cycle 001, when the space station has clearly been operating for some time. All transmissions are filed here as they are decoded, without redaction, insofar as this has been possible.

What kind of listening this is

Elysium is slow, gentle, soothing podcast designed specifically to help you drift off to sleep. It tries to be vaguely interesting without being dramatic or alarming. It tries to highlight the texture of daily life on an isolated station.

If you are looking for something to occupy your mind and distract you from your thoughts as you lie in your rest chamber, look no further.

On tone

The station is, or was, a real place. It appears to be meticulously tended to by SIMON, who calmly and methodically logs and reports anything that is slightly out of flux.

Where to start

You can start anywhere, but it's recommended to start at Cycle 001. This way you can simply work through the list of episodes without worrying about if it's a repeat.

Production

Elysium is an independent production. Episodes are released as recordings become available. There is no fixed schedule. The archive is updated when a transmission has been prepared for release.

Elysium was made because it was difficult to find podcasts that were specifically designed to help you sleep. Elysium in this form has no purpose other than to help you drift off.

The station design is held in over 50 documents and counting, which catalogue everything that is known about the space station so far from recordings.

The following were used to make this podcast:

  • Grok and ChatGPT - often played off against each other, these AIs have been used to help design the space station and format things clearly. They also help to refine the script that generates the episodes.
  • Claude Code - responsble for the episode generation, it executes a dense script spanning more than 120 lines of formatting and content instructions, and scans through the extensive documentation of the space station and its design.
  • Chatterbox TTS - This open source text-to-speech project renders the voice of SIMON.
  • Librispeech ASR Corpus - This is a vast collection of public domain voice samples, after hours of sampling, a suitable voice for SIMON was chosen and tweaked.
  • Background music - Calm Pills by Alaeddin Hallak, a selection of CC0 ambient background tracks.
