Station Elysium — Project Information

About

What Elysium is, what kind of listening it offers, and where to begin.

The project

Elysium is a serialised sequence of audio transmissions from SIMON — the Station Interactive Monitor responsible for overseeing daily operations aboard the space station Elysium. Each episode is one cycle's broadcast: announcements, safety notices, logistics updates, and maintenance reports, filed as they were received.

How long the station has been in continuous operation is unknown. Its location is unknown. Why the transmissions begin at Cycle 001, when the station has clearly been running for some time, is also unknown. All transmissions are filed here as they are decoded, without redaction, insofar as this has been possible.

What kind of listening this is

Elysium is slow and gentle. It is designed to occupy the mind without demanding anything of it — softly spoken fragments that catalogue the texture of daily life on a distant, isolated station. Nothing dramatic. Nothing alarming. Just the quiet accumulation of ordinary things.

If you need something to keep your thoughts at a distance as you settle in for the night, this is made for that.

On tone

The station was — or is — a real place, meticulously maintained. SIMON logs and reports everything that strays even slightly from the expected. The routine is the point. What lies beneath it emerges slowly, if at all.

Where to start

You can begin anywhere, but Cycle 001 is recommended. Starting at the beginning means you can work through the archive in order, without wondering what you might have missed.

Production

Elysium is an independent production. Episodes are released as recordings become available — there is no fixed schedule. The archive is updated when a transmission has been prepared.

It was made because sleep-oriented audio was difficult to find in the specific form Elysium takes: no narrative tension, no hooks, no cliffhangers. Just a place to be for a while.

The station itself is documented across more than fifty working files, cataloguing everything known from the recordings so far.

Tools used

  • Grok and ChatGPT — used in combination to help develop the station's design and refine the episode generation process.
  • Claude Code — responsible for episode generation, executing a detailed script of over 120 formatting and content instructions against the station documentation.
  • Chatterbox TTS — the open-source text-to-speech system used to render SIMON's voice.
  • LibriSpeech ASR Corpus — a public domain voice sample library from which SIMON's voice profile was selected and refined after extended testing.
  • Calm Pills — Alaeddin Hallak — a selection of CC0-licensed ambient background tracks used throughout the series.
