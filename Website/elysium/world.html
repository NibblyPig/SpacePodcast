Station Archive — Selected Reference Material

Station Elysium

A curated selection of archival material. This is not a complete record. Full operational dossiers remain classified under Maintenance Authority protocols.

Station Overview

General access — filed Cycle 001

Designation

Elysium, Orbital Platform Seven. One of four platforms in the outer maintenance corridor. Commissioned under a joint logistics and research mandate. Current operational classification: active. Functional status as of Cycle 031: under review.

Position

Fixed orbit at the outer perimeter of the territorial boundary. Approximately fourteen light-days from the nearest inhabited body. Resupply transits operate on a forty-day rotation under standard conditions. Conditions have not been standard for some time.

Crew complement

Classified. Station population at the time of the Interval is not confirmed in any publicly accessible document. Internal records suggest a permanent staff of between sixty and ninety individuals, with a variable number of transit and contractor personnel at any given time.

Structure

The station is organised into five operational decks. Decks one through three contain crew quarters, medical, administration, and common areas. Deck four is engineering and environmental systems. Deck five is listed in public records as storage. Access to Deck five requires a Grade III clearance or higher.

Cycles and Routines

Station time — internal operations

The station cycle

Station time is measured in cycles rather than standard calendar units. One cycle is approximately equivalent to thirty standard days. The cycle system was adopted early in the station's operational history and has not been revised. All internal filing, scheduling, and incident records use cycle notation.

Daily structure

The station operates on a three-shift rotation: day, mid, and night. Environmental systems simulate a twenty-four-hour light cycle. Most crew members report that this simulation becomes less convincing after several months. There is no official response on record to this observation.

Resupply transits

Supply vessels are scheduled to arrive every forty days under standard protocol. A transit vessel from the maintenance authority arrives every ninety days for inspection and personnel rotation. Several of the transmissions in this archive concern vessels that arrived unexpectedly, or not at all.

Irregularities

Noted discrepancies — partial record

The environmental log alterations

Manual alterations to environmental monitoring records were first noted in Cycle 001. The alterations affected readings in the secondary maintenance corridor. A source has not been confirmed. The incident is referenced in seventeen subsequent transmissions without resolution.

Personnel manifest discrepancies

Beginning in Cycle 005, incoming vessel manifests do not consistently match departure records filed at the origin station. The discrepancies are not identical in character and do not appear to follow a pattern. Maintenance Authority has not responded to formal queries on this matter.

The Interval

An event referred to in internal records as the Interval occurred at a point after the transmissions currently archived. The nature of the Interval is not described in any document available for general access. The archive exists because of it. Further material will be filed as it becomes available.
