# Pamela Fountain

## Type
Landmark / Social Node

## Location
Central Spine — Mid Section (adjacent to Aurelian Gallery)

## Overview

Pamela Fountain is a zero-gravity water installation located along a widened section of the central spine.

A contained volume of water is suspended within a controlled field, forming a slow-moving, continuously shifting structure.

At its centre is a fixed sculptural form, around which the water gathers and reshapes.

It has become an informal meeting point for residents moving through the spine.

---

## Characteristics

- suspended water mass held in a stable containment field
- central sculptural figure partially enveloped by the water
- slow internal motion producing shifting reflections
- perimeter lighting that changes intensity across the cycle
- open standing space surrounding the installation

---

## Human Activity Indicators

- brief pauses and informal gatherings around the perimeter
- repeated use as a reference point for meeting
- fluctuating density of people without formal scheduling

---

## Persistent Details

- the shape of the water mass subtly changes over time without a fixed pattern
- reflections from the fountain occasionally extend further into the corridor than expected
- the central figure is not always fully visible depending on the water’s configuration
- the surrounding area remains consistently more occupied than adjacent spine sections

---

## Notes

- the identity of the figure is not referenced in station documentation
- useful as a named meeting reference (“meet at the fountain”)
- supports soft human presence without direct description
- can be referenced across many episodes without needing variation
