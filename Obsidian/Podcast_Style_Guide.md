# Podcast Style Guide

## Core Tone
Calm, steady, slightly warm.
Measured and composed.
Never dramatic, never poetic.

## Core Principles
- Every line must have a purpose.
- Prefer practical, resident-relevant information.
- Avoid exposition.
- Avoid explaining how the station works unless necessary.

## Presence Ambiguity Rule (CRITICAL)
The broadcast must NEVER directly confirm that residents are present.

Allowed:
- inferred activity
- expected patterns
- environmental observations

Forbidden:
- direct statements of people doing things

Bad:
"people are already moving through the corridors"

Good:
"the corridors are beginning to show early-cycle activity"

## Information Style
- Localised, not global
- Specific where useful
- Avoid vague terms like "nominal"

## Variation
- Mix operational info with soft human-layer reminders
- Include occasional gentle suggestions for wellbeing

## Flow
- No rigid sections
- Natural drift between topics
