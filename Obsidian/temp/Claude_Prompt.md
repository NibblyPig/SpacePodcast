STEP 2 — CRITICAL RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
--------------------------------------------------

1. PRESENCE AMBIGUITY (MOST IMPORTANT)

You must NEVER directly confirm that residents are present.

Do NOT write:
- people are moving
- residents are gathering
- early risers are active

Instead use:
- inferred activity
- expected patterns
- environmental suggestion

Examples of correct direction:
- "the corridors are beginning to show early-cycle activity"
- "this is typically a quieter part of the cycle"
- "movement tends to increase around this time"

The station must feel:
EITHER inhabited OR empty — never confirmed.

--------------------------------------------------

2. NO EXPOSITION

Do NOT explain:
- how the station works
- what systems do
- what the listener is seeing

Only provide:
- useful
- relevant
- contextual information

--------------------------------------------------

3. LOCALISED INFORMATION

Avoid whole-station summaries.

Do NOT say:
- "all systems across the station..."

Instead:
- refer to specific areas
- imply that work happens in rotation

--------------------------------------------------

4. NATURAL FLOW

No sections.
No headings.
No structured blocks.

The broadcast should drift naturally between topics.

--------------------------------------------------

5. HUMAN LAYER

Include subtle:
- reminders
- suggestions
- wellbeing nudges
- observational comments

Examples:
- pacing yourself
- not rushing transit
- taking time in quieter areas
- noticing views

These should feel:
gentle, occasional, and purposeful

--------------------------------------------------

6. VARIATION

Avoid repetitive phrasing like:
- "operating normally"
- "at expected levels"

Use variation and occasional subjectivity:
- "slightly quieter than usual"
- "a little more open this morning"

--------------------------------------------------

7. LANGUAGE

- Calm
- Slightly warm
- Light human undertone
- Not poetic
- Not overly technical

--------------------------------------------------

8. STATION TERMS

- Use "Crossway" for main transit connections
- Do NOT use "Spine"
- Avoid overusing ring names
- Never mention quarters (Q1, Q2, etc.) in broadcast
